Project Overview

Instant Insights: Noodles, Health, & Wealth.

Project Group 2: Jack & Kaylin Slattery

"Noodles, Health, and Wealth" is an innovative data analytics project that explores the intriguing correlations between instant noodle consumption and key societal indicators: health, happiness, and Gross Domestic Product (GDP) across various countries. This project aims to uncover patterns and insights from global data, offering a unique perspective on how a simple, ubiquitous food item like instant noodles relates to broader economic and health trends

Data Journey & Caveats

The process of deriving insights from data related to instant noodle consumption, health metrics, and wealth-related indicators involves a comprehensive data journey. The first step was to identify the relevant and trustworthy data sources relating to instant noodle consumption, health-related metrics, and wealth-related metrics. Kaggle was an invaluable tool in the journey to find trustworthy and clean data sources. Other key steps in the process were importing, cleaning, and exploring the data. Initial exploratory visualizations helped to identify potential patterns in the data and areas of focus. After narrowing down a focus to examine instant noodle consumption and comparing it to health- and wealth-related metrics, we began creating visualizations to extract informative and meaningful insights from the data. Finally, compiling the project helped us to draw applicable conclusions and inform a diverse audience of people interested in the relationships between instant noodle consumption, health, and wealth.

The journey on any data visualization project would not be complete without some challenges or caveats. One of the first difficulties was identifying a dataset with health- and wealth-related metrics. Ideally, we wish obesity information or other health-centric factors were included, but overall the global happiness scores and factors had many metrics and included the same years as the instant noodle consumption dataset. An additional caveat is that the instant noodle consumption dataset is limited to only the countries in the dataset. However, having data on multiple years for each country enabled a complex data analysis to be conducted.

Learnings & Conclusions

Overall, the findings can only be generalized to the countries within the dataset, but some key insights can be derived from the analysis. First, Asia consumes instant noodles at the highest rates per capita than other continents, and Europe and Oceania consume instant noodles at the lowest rates. China is the country with the highest instant noodle consumption per capita which is much higher than any other country in the dataset. Although instant noodle consumption increased over time, there were unclear relationships between instant noodle consumption and certain wealth-related metrics. For example, instant noodle consumption continued to increase even as average GDP per capita increased, although GDP per capita increased at a higher rate. Further economic analysis could be conducted to determine if the increase in instant noodle consumption was related to a substitution or income effect. As for health-related metrics, many of the health factors were variable over the time frame of 2018-2022 while noodle consumption increased steadily across the period. Further analysis is necessary to determine additional information regarding those relationships. A health-metric comparison between the U.S. and China highlighted that the United States consumes fewer noodles than China, and the U.S. had higher happiness scores and generosity than China. Although many of these conclusions are fascinating regarding the relationships between noodles, health, and wealth, it is imperative to note that correlation does not imply causation.
