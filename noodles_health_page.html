Noodles x Health

Have you ever wondered how instant noodle consumption relates to health?

This page provides various information and insights regarding instant noodle consumption and its relationship to health-related metrics.

Health Topic 1

How do various health metrics compare across levels of noodle consumption?

    1. For countries that consume a lot of noodles, social support recieves relatively higher scores than than happiness score.
    2. Generosity scores are relatively lowest in countries that consume a large amount of noodles.
    3. Generally, for countries that consume the most amount of instant noodles, healthy life expectancy and freedom to make life choices are the highest metrics when compared with happiness, social support and generosity.

Health Topic 2

How do healthy life expectancy and noodle consumption change from 2018 to 2022?

    1. Over the time period, healthy life expectancy starts to increase, but then takes a significant drop in 2020, whereas the average noodle consumption sharply increases over the time frame.
    2. Average Noodle consumption increased heavily from 2019 to 2022, which may be a result of the Covid-19 pandemic.
    3. Average Healthy Life expectancy reached its peak in 2019 while average noodle consumption reached its highest level in 2022.

Health Topic 3

How do happiness scores and noodle consumption change from 2018 to 2022?

    1. Over the time period, happiness scores are rather variable as scores drop, then start to rise, and then drop sharply again.
    2. Average Noodle consumption increased heavily from 2019 to 2022 as seen by the upwards-sloping line.
    3. Average happiness reached its lowest point in 2022 which is when average noodle consumption reached its highest point.

Health Topic 4

How do various health metrics compare for the United States and China?

    1. Noodle consumption remained relatively constant in the United States while China's increased, but overall China consumes many more noodles per capita than the United States.
    2. Happiness scores remained relatively constant in the United States around 6.85 whereas China's began to increase after 2020 to nearly 5.5.
    3. Social support and healthy life expectancy followed relatively similar patters with increases and decreases in the same years for both respective countries.
    4. China's generosity increased from 0.029 in 2018 to 0.099 while the United States generosity decreased from 0.291 in 2018 to 0.22 in 2022.
